Sorin Cadar,
UI Designer with a user-centered mindset

I am an open minded creative with a sensitive personality that makes me pretty empathic and self aware. I enjoy solving real-life problems using my visual design skills and my eye for aesthetics.

My journey into the visual design field began 12 years ago. For the last 8 years I've been chanelling my creativity and analytical and critical thinking into creating digital products that generate positive feelings in users by assisting them in completing their tasks with the least possible effort, thus helping clients to reach their business goals.

I am a self-taught digital designer with a Bachelor Degree in Graphic Design. I delight in creating and experimenting while discovering new things about the world. I am intrigued by psychology and human behaviour so I enjoy all opportunities to learn more about these subjects.

I am currently working at Redouble Agency in Arad, Romania but looking to relocate into a bike-friendly country and starting a new chapter in my career.

For me, working in a team is the most fun. I believe that the creative process is stronger when many different perspectives are involved.

Other than my passion towards design and problem solving, I like to enjoy life at its fullest. I seek work-life balance and I care deeply about my physical and mental health. My greatest passions are: travelling, hiking, cycling, mixing music and dancing at great music events.

How I Do It

My creative process is mainly based on the Double Diamond model. I am also inspired by other models such as IBM Loop, Design Thinking and The Five Levels of UX.

My creative process is mainly based on the Double Diamond model. I am also inspired by other models such as IBM Loop, Design Thinking and The Five Levels of UX.

I use the four big stages of the design process to organize my work logically and to allow my thoughts to flow in a natural order. The process is not linear in any way. Understanding and creativity manifest different in every project, so it is normal to go back and forth between this stages.

1

Discovery
& Definition

Discovery & Definition

At the beginning of every project I aim to understand the problem and the client's vision. After my team discusses with the client and the stakeholders we usually have a discovery meeting.

Then I analyze the initial information and I continue by doing my own research abouth the client's industry, the target audience as well as the relevant competitors. Immersing myself into the subject of the project I can identify the gap in information and come up with the right questions. When the research is done I meet with my team again to discuss how to fill that gap.

At the end of this phase I aim to have a clear definition of the project scope, the business and the product goals, the user needs and pains as well as a estimated timeline of the project.

2

Ideation
& Validation

Ideation & Validation

When the guidelines of the project are clear I begin the ideation process. I start brainstorming with pen and paper, making very rough sketches of various ideas and solutions. Then I translate the ones that make the most sense into low-fidelity wireframes. To see if the functionalities support the user's and business' goals I prefer to connect all the screens together and develop a low-fi prototype.

Following the functional specifications I proceed with developing the wireframe further by adding the information architecture and the content that I receive from my team. The end result of this phase represents the high fidelity wireframe-prototype of the product that focuses on the functionality and user interaction, offering only small hints about the layout and totally ignoring the look and feel.

For me it is very important to validate the product with my team, the client, the stakeholders and the users at this early stage when changes are cheaper and take less time.

3

Visual Design

Visual Design

With a clear blueprint of the product I can enter the visual design phase. At first I do visual research aiming to build a moodboard that communicates the overall direction for the look and feel and to collect valuable UI inspiration.

I continue with visual design exploration, a back-and-forth process between defining a style guide and discovering multiple possible visual directions  based on the previously created moodboard. After the exploration I present my findings to the team and decide together on the way to go forward.

In the end I start designing the screens of the product at high fidelity, following the plan layed out during the previous steps  and phases. I usually build a high-fidelity prototype too, illustrating the interaction design through micro-interactions and screen transitions. The interactive prototype is used in testing and finally it is approved by the client and the stakeholders.

4

Handoff

Handoff

In the last phase of my design process I am making sure to transfer all the necessary materials to the developers that are implementing the product.

For a smooth handoff I deliver a developer-friendly design, with all the layers grouped and named according to the HTML structure and all the components built on the implementation model.

To assure a high fidelity development at all resolutions I investigate the design's responsiveness by adapting each screen at multiple resolutions in order to illustrate the changes at certain breakpoints and the resize behavior between them.

After I finish the design I am creating the final Style Guide, a single source of truth that documents visual design characteristics like colors and their usecases, typography styles, spacings, shadows and UI components.
